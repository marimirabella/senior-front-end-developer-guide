## Declarative vs Imperative programming

- **Declarative** - expresses the logic of a computation without describing its control flow. Abstracts the flow control process and instead spend lines of code describing. Uses expressions like `map/reduce/filter`.<br/>
  Answers the question _“What code does”_. It is like asking your friend to fix your car. You do not care how to fix it, that is up to your friend.
- **Imperative** - spends lines of code describing the specific steps used to achieve the desired results. Uses statements like `for, if, switch`.<br/>
  Answers the question - _“How to do things”_. It is like your friend calling his father and his father telling him how to fix your car step by step.
